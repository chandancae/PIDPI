The PIDPI Act provides a legal framework for:

1. Protection of Whistleblowers:
Ensures that individuals who report wrongdoing are protected from retaliation, harassment, or victimization.

2. Reporting Mechanisms:
Establishes proper channels for reporting corruption, misconduct, or violation of law or rules.

3. Investigation Procedures:
Defines the process for investigating complaints and taking appropriate action.

4. Transparency and Accountability:
Promotes a culture of openness and accountability in public institutions.

Key Features:

‚Ä¢ Confidentiality of complainant's identity

‚Ä¢ Time-bound investigation process

‚Ä¢ Appeal mechanisms

‚Ä¢ Penalty provisions for false complaints

Reporting Process:

Step 1: Identification
Identify acts of corruption, misconduct, or violation that warrant reporting.

Step 2: Documentation
Gather relevant evidence and documentation to support your complaint.

Step 3: Filing Complaint
Submit complaint through designated channels with proper details.

Step 4: Investigation
Competent authority conducts investigation within specified timeframe.

Do's:

‚Ä¢ Provide accurate and complete information

‚Ä¢ Maintain confidentiality during investigation

Don'ts:
